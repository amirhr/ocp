# Patent Policy

## Pledge

Every contributor to the Open Conversion Pingback (OCP) specification, by submitting an issue, pull request, or comment containing technical content (a "Contribution"), irrevocably grants to anyone who implements this specification a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, no-charge, royalty-free, irrevocable patent license under any patent claims they own or control, now or in the future, that are necessarily infringed by implementing the specification as published.

This grant covers patent claims that read on the specification as a whole and on any individual Contribution that is incorporated into the specification.

## Defensive termination

If any party initiates patent litigation (including a cross-claim or counterclaim in a lawsuit) alleging that this specification, or any implementation of it, constitutes direct or contributory patent infringement, then any patent licenses granted under this policy to that party for this specification shall terminate as of the date such litigation is filed.

## Non-assertion

Whimful FZ-LLC, the initial author, and all subsequent contributors agree not to assert any patent claims they own or control against any party for implementing the specification as published, except as necessary for defensive termination above.

## Intent

This policy is modeled on the patent provisions of the Apache License 2.0 and the Open Web Foundation Agreement 1.0. The intent is unambiguous:

1. The specification is permanently royalty-free for everyone.
2. No contributor (including the original author and sponsors) can later use a patent to extract value from implementers.
3. Implementers are protected from patent retaliation by other implementers.
4. The defensive termination clause discourages patent attacks against the specification or its implementations.

## Scope

This policy covers the specification text. Reference implementations and other code published in the OCP repository are licensed under the Apache License 2.0 (see `LICENSE-CODE`), which contains its own patent grant with equivalent effect.

## Defensive publication

This specification is published openly to establish prior art. The intent is that the techniques described herein be permanently available to the public domain of ideas, such that no party (whether a contributor, an implementer, or an unrelated third party) can later claim exclusive patent rights over them.
